Task 3 — Integrated Academic30s prep / 60s resp
Using the examples from the lecture, explain the concept of alarm calls.
Reading Passage (45s):
Alarm Calls are warning signals produced by animals to alert other members of their species about nearby predators. These calls can benefit the group but often put the caller at increased risk by drawing the predator's attention.
Lecture Transcript: Let's consider two examples of alarm calls. First, vervet monkeys produce different alarm calls for different predators — one for leopards, another for eagles, and a third for snakes. When a vervet hears a leopard call, it runs into trees. For an eagle call, it looks up and hides in bushes. This shows that alarm calls can carry specific information. Second, consider the prairie dog. Prairie dogs give alarm calls when predators approach, and interestingly, they can even describe the predator's size, color, and speed. However, by calling, the prairie dog makes itself more visible and vulnerable to the predator, which is a significant cost.

Task 4 — Integrated Audio Only20s prep / 60s resp
Using points and examples from the lecture, explain the concept of surrogate advertising.
Lecture Transcript (Audio Simulation): Today we'll discuss the concept of surrogate advertising. This is a marketing strategy used when a company cannot directly advertise a particular product, often due to legal restrictions. Instead, the company promotes a different, associated product or brand that carries the same name. For example, in many countries, tobacco advertising is banned on television. So tobacco companies create advertisements for other products — like clothing, accessories, or even social events — that feature their brand name prominently. When consumers see these ads, they associate the brand with the restricted product. Another example is alcohol companies sponsoring sports teams or music festivals. They cannot advertise the alcohol directly during certain broadcasts, but their brand logo appears on team jerseys and event banners, keeping the brand visible to the public.
