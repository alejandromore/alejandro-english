Task 2 — Integrated Speaking (Campus Situation)

Read a short passage (45s) → Listen to a conversation (simulated) → 30s prep → 60s response

Practice Item 1

Reading Passage — 45 seconds
"The university has announced a new policy requiring all first-year students to live on campus. Administrators believe this will help students build stronger social connections and improve academic performance. The policy will take effect next fall semester."
45
Conversation (Simulated Audio Transcript)
Man: Did you hear about the new housing policy? Woman: Yeah, and I think it's a terrible idea. First, it forces students to pay for dorm housing, which is often more expensive than off-campus options. Many students rely on cheaper apartments nearby. Also, living off campus teaches independence — managing your own cooking, cleaning, and budgeting. The university shouldn't take that away.
Prompt
The woman expresses her opinion about the new housing policy. State her opinion and explain the reasons she gives for holding that opinion.

Task 3 — Integrated Speaking (Academic)

Read a short passage (45s) → Listen to a lecture excerpt (simulated) → 30s prep → 60s response

Practice Item 1

Reading Passage — 45 seconds
"Cognitive Dissonance is a psychological theory that describes the mental discomfort experienced when a person holds two conflicting beliefs or attitudes simultaneously. To reduce this discomfort, people often change their beliefs, justify their actions, or avoid situations that create conflict."
45
Lecture Excerpt (Simulated Audio Transcript)
So, let's look at a classic example. Imagine a student who knows that smoking is harmful to health, but continues to smoke anyway. This creates cognitive dissonance — the student knows the facts but acts against them. To reduce the discomfort, the student might convince themselves that the research is exaggerated, or that they'll quit soon, or they might avoid reading health articles altogether. Another example: a person who values environmental conservation but drives a large, fuel-inefficient car. They might justify it by saying they need it for work, or they might donate to environmental causes to offset the guilt.
Prompt
Using the examples from the lecture, explain the concept of cognitive dissonance.

Practice Item 2

Reading Passage — 45 seconds
"The Hawthorne Effect refers to the phenomenon where people modify their behavior when they know they are being observed. This effect was first identified during studies at a factory in the 1920s, where workers increased productivity simply because researchers were watching them."
45
Lecture Excerpt (Simulated Audio Transcript)
The original study took place at the Hawthorne Works factory. Researchers wanted to see if changing lighting conditions would affect productivity. Surprisingly, productivity increased regardless of whether lighting was improved or worsened. The key factor was that workers knew they were being studied, so they worked harder. We see this in modern contexts too. For instance, in a software company, developers might write cleaner code when they know their work is being reviewed by peers. Or in a hospital, staff might follow hygiene protocols more carefully when they know inspectors are present.
Prompt
Using the examples from the lecture, explain the Hawthorne Effect.

Example Model Answer — Task 2, Item 1

"The university has announced a new policy requiring first-year students to live on campus. However, she argues that dorm housing is often more expensive than off-campus options, and many students rely on cheaper apartments nearby. Additionally, she points out that living off campus teaches students independence, such as managing their own cooking, cleaning, and budgeting. Therefore, she believes the university should not take that choice away from students."

Highlights

She argues — opinion statement (green)
