# 🎯 PÁRRAFO DE PRÁCTICA — Palabras problemáticas

## Instrucciones:
1. Lee el párrafo en voz alta 3 veces (grabar en Voice Coach)
2. Enfatiza MUY BIEN las consonantes finales de las palabras en **negrita**
3. Di "looking FOR" como una sola unidad — no te saltes el "for"
4. Para "the USA", siempre di "the" antes de "USA"

---

## PÁRRAFO DE PRÁCTICA

When **big** **executives** from **the USA** come to Latin America, they are **looking for** **competitive** **salaries** and **others** who can speak **Portuguese**. Last year, I **decided** to pursue a **career** in a **corporate** **business negotiation** team, and **currently** I am studying **chemistry** and English. It is a **truthful** fact that **through** hard work, you can achieve your goals.

---

## Lista de palabras a practicar (en orden de dificultad):

| # | Palabra | Problema | Cómo practicarla |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **big** | /g/ final se pierde | big-BIG-big (exagerar la /g/) |
| 2 | **executives** | -s final se pierde | ex-e-cu-TIVE-S (marcar las dos sílabas finales) |
| 3 | **the USA** | "the" se omite | decir "the USA" como chunk, nunca "USA" solo |
| 4 | **looking for** | "for" se traga | LOOKING-FOR (decirlo como una palabra) |
| 5 | **competitive** | /r/ + /t/ difícil | com-PET-i-TIVE (separar sílabas) |
| 6 | **salaries** | /l/ + /r/ confusión | SA-la-RIES (exagerar la L y la R) |
| 7 | **others** | -s final se pierde | o-THER-S (marcar la S final) |
| 8 | **Portuguese** | spelling + pronunciación | POR-tu-GUESE (exagerar GUESE) |
| 9 | **decided** | -d final se pierde | de-CI-DED (marcar la D final) |
| 10 | **career** | spelling | ca-REER (exagerar la R final) |
| 11 | **corporate** | spelling | COR-po-RATE (3 sílabas claras) |
| 12 | **business negotiation** | frase malinterpretada | BUS-i-ness NE-go-ti-A-tion (lento 10x) |
| 13 | **currently** | /r/ inicial difícil | CUR-rent-ly (exagerar la R) |
| 14 | **chemistry** | /k/ + /s/ | CHE-mis-TRY (exagerar TRY) |
| 15 | **truthful** | /θ/ + /f/ | TRUTH-FUL (exagerar ambos sonidos) |
| 16 | **through** | /θ/ | THROUGH (morder la lengua suavemente) |

---

## Versión para Voice Coach (sin negrita):

When big executives from the USA come to Latin America, they are looking for competitive salaries and others who can speak Portuguese. Last year, I decided to pursue a career in a corporate business negotiation team, and currently I am studying chemistry and English. It is a truthful fact that through hard work, you can achieve your goals.
